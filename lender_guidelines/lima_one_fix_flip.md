## Eligible Property Types

- SFR, 2–4 unit
- Warrantable condos
- Townhomes

**Not eligible:** Ground-up new construction (use Lima One New Construction product), rural, manufactured homes, commercial.

---

## Borrower Experience Requirement

Lima One requires documented experience for Fix & Flip:

| Experience Level | Max LTV ARV | Max LTC |
|---|---|---|
| 0 projects completed | 65% ARV, 80% LTC | 80% |
| 1–2 projects | 70% ARV, 85% LTC | 85% |
| 3+ projects | 75% ARV, 90% LTC | 90% |

Experience is documented via: closing HUDs, previous project portfolios, or entity track record letter from CPA/title company.

---

## Renovation Budget

- Line-item budget required (trade-by-trade breakdown)
- GC must be licensed + insured in property state
- GC W9 required
- Lima One retains holdback on renovation budget (released in draws)
- Draw inspections required per draw (3–4 draws typical)
- Draw fee: $350/draw (borrower pays)
- Contingency: 10% of renovation budget minimum

## Standard Conditions Package

**Prior to Submission:**
1. Tri-merge credit
2. Executed purchase contract
3. Detailed line-item renovation budget
4. GC license + insurance certificate
5. Entity docs (LLC/LP — same as DSCR requirements)
6. Borrower experience documentation

**Prior to Close:**
7. As-Is + ARV appraisal (full 1004 or 1073 for condo)
8. Title commitment
9. Insurance binder (builder's risk or landlord policy acceptable)
10. Bank statements — 2 months (reserves + down payment sourcing)

**At Closing:**
11. Signed Draw Schedule Agreement
12. Executed scope of work with GC signature

---

## Hot Buttons

- **GC docs cause most stalls:** Get the GC license + insurance cert before submitting. Lima One will not order the appraisal without them.
- **ARV is the hard cap:** If the appraiser comes in lower than expected, LTC/LTV will be recalculated. Budget contingency or have down payment ready to cover gap.
- **No inexperienced first-time investors at max LTC:** Experience tier drives LTC. Don't over-promise leverage to a borrower with zero experience.
- **Draw timeline:** Allow 3–5 business days per draw inspection. Delays in renovation extend carrying costs — factor into borrower's budget.
